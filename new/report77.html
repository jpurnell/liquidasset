 COMMENT:

  The noses of these Zinfandel wines were very different; there was a range from      
  rubbery to fruity. That had an impact on the judges' evaluation of the individual      
  wines. Extraordinary diversity somewhat driven by age differences but     
  equally a function of stylistic considerations. The wines ranged from     
  spicy to jammy to hot or alcoholic. It is interesting that the wine that  
  was deemed the best was the most alcoholic (16.8%) and these high alcohol 
  contents cast question on the judgments rendered above. The older wines in
  this tasting either become more Burgundy-like (i.e. Ridge) or port-like   
  (i.e., Hop Kiln or Rosenblum).
