 COMMENT:

  Even before the ranking were obtained, one taster said that these wines
  were awesomely majestic. Although it was extremely difficult to
  rank-order these wines, it was quite surprising that the correlation
  among the tasters was statistically highly significant. Several tasters
  thought that the 1983s were fading ever so slightly. Nevertheless, these
  wines were deemed to be about as good as wines ever get. The 1983s should
  probably be drunk now. The 1985s have none of the herbal character that
  some tasters find annoying (and others like), while the 1988s seem very
  concentrated. Robert Parker says "the 1988s are even richer than the
  extraordinary 1985s and more concentrated than the magnificent 1983s."
  We agree with this assessment, and we don't always agree by any means.
  These wines combine the extraordinary softness and texture of the greatest
  Burgundies with the intensity and extract of the greatest French, Italian
  and California cabernets. Note that all the wines received 100 points from
  Parker, except for the 1983 La Landonne, which received 98+. We
  finally note that some people expected big differences across the
  vineyards, while the big differences were actually across the
  vintages.
